Japanese Archery

Japanese Archery is a martial art of Japan, you can explore more below!

Japanese Archery a.k.a Kyūdō. According to Wikipedia, Kyūdō (Japanese: 弓道) is the Japanese martial art of archery. Kyūdō is based on kyūjutsu ("art of archery"), which originated with the samurai class of feudal Japan. In 1919, the name of kyūjutsu was officially changed to kyūdō, and following the example of other martial arts that have been systematizing for educational purposes, kyūdō also reorganized and integrated various forms of shooting that had been used up until then.(you can find the references and more information below)

As Archery, this martial art requires two basic equipment: bow and arrow The Yumi (弓, lit. the "[Japanese] Bow") is exceptionally tall (standing over two metres), surpassing the height of the archer. Ya (矢, lit. "[Japanese] Arrow") shafts (Yagara (簳, lit. "Arrow Shaft")) are traditionally made of bamboo(but nowdays some arrow shafts are made of aluminium or carbon fibres), with either eagle, hawk or turkey feathers (Hane (羽, lit. "Feather(s)")). The kyūdō archer also wears a glove on the right hand, called a Yugake (弽, lit. "Yumi gloves"). There are many varieties of yugake; they are typically made of deerskin. you can find these equipment in the background image.(also, you can find more information and references below.)
